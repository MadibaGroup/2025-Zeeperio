\begin{abstract}
Many cryptographic end-to-end election verification (E2E-V) protocols allow the public to verify proofs, but participation in this important process is largely left as an {\em opt-in}, {\em adhoc} exercise. We present \textsc{Zeeperio}, a modern update to the Eperio protocol toward making election proof verification routine and automatic. Zeeperio is zk-SNARK system that verifies proofs automatically via smart contracts, improving on Eperio's linear-complexity proofs through a novel, succinct argument with size and verification time constant in the number of ballots. 

We implemented Zeeperio and tested it on election with $\mathcal{B}=100,000$ ballots and $\mathcal{C}=5$ candidates and demonstrate verification on Ethereum L1 for under $\$60 \mathsf{USD}$ (at the time of writing), using the precompiles introduced by EIPs 196 and 197. In comparison to a leading STARK-based implementation, which required proofs in the Megabyte range for $32,000$ ballots and took $9$ days to generate, Zeeperio's proof for $100,000$ ballots measures in the kilobyte range and was completed in $3$ hours on a standard CPU architecture. These orders-of-magnitude improvements to performance directly translate to lower transaction costs and improved on-chain scalability for larger elections, toward making routine, automated election verification and interesting and practical possibility.

\end{abstract}
