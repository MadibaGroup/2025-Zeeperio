\begin{abstract}

Many cryptographic end-to-end election verification (E2E-V) protocols allow the public to verify proofs, but participation in this important process is largely left as an opt-in, ad hoc exercise. 
We present \textsc{Zeeperio}, a modern update to the Eperio protocol toward making election proof verification routine and automatic. 
Zeeperio is a custom zk-SNARK system that verifies proofs automatically via smart contracts, improving on Eperio's linear-complexity through a novel, succinct argument with constant-size proofs in the number of ballots, and inexpensive on-chain verification.
We implemented Zeeperio and tested it on an election with $100{,}000$ ballots and $5$ candidates. Additionally, we demonstrated verification on Ethereum Sepolia testnet for under $\$15\,$USD (at time of writing), using precompiles introduced in EIPs 196 and 197. 
By comparison to a leading STARK-based implementation, which required proofs in the megabyte range for $32{,}000$ ballots and prover time of $13{,}409$ core-minutes, Zeeperio's proof for $100{,}000$ ballots measures in the kilobyte range and was completed in under $5$ hours on a MacBook Pro (M3 Pro, 18 GB RAM). 
These orders-of-magnitude improvements to performance directly translate to lower transaction costs and improved on-chain scalability for larger elections, toward making routine, automated election verification an interesting and practical possibility.

\end{abstract}
